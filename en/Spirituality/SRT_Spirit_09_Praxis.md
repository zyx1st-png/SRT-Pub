## ¬ß1 Getting Started: Your First 30 Days

### ¬ß1.1 Week 1: Establishing a Baseline

**Goal**: Not "practicing," but **observing**.

**Task**:

#### Day 1-7: Self-Observation Journal

Spend 10 minutes every night writing down‚Äî

1. **Emotion**: What was your primary feeling today?
2. **Thoughts**: What occupied your mind mostly?
3. **Body**: Any tension or discomfort?
4. **Ratings** (1-10):
   - Stress Level
   - Happiness
   - Quality of Focus
   - Compassion

**Why?**

You need a **baseline**‚Äîyou must know where you are now to see progress later.

**SRT**: Establishing a rough measurement of $\theta_{\text{baseline}}$.

---

### ¬ß1.2 Week 2: Minimum Viable Practice

**Goal**: Establish a **minimal** but **sustainable** habit.

**Protocol**: **5-Minute Breath Meditation**

**Time**: Daily, at a fixed time (Recommended: right after waking up).

**Steps**:
1. Sit down (chair or floor).
2. Close your eyes.
3. Focus on the sensation of breathing at the nostrils.
4. Distracted ‚Üí Notice it ‚Üí Return to the breath.
5. Open eyes after 5 minutes.

**Rules**: 
- ‚úÖ 5 minutes every single day, even if the quality is poor.
- ‚ùå Do not "wait until you have time" or "wait for a good mood".
- ‚ùå Do not expect special experiences.

**Why only 5 minutes?**

$$P(\text{Consistency}) \propto \frac{1}{T_{\text{required}}}$$

You **can definitely do** 5 minutes‚Äîbuilding the habit is more important than the duration.

---

### ¬ß1.3 Week 3: Fine-Tuning

**Now**: You have 7 days of 5-minute practice.

**Observation**: 
- How hard was it?
- What was the biggest obstacle?

**Adjustment Options**:

| If... | Then... |
|:--------|:--------|
| Too easy | Increase to 10 minutes |
| Can't sit still | Try walking meditation |
| Keep falling asleep | Do it in the morning, sit up straight |
| Bored | Try a body scan |
| Increased anxiety | Normal, just continue |

---

### ¬ß1.4 Week 4: Integration

**Goal**: Extend practice into daily life.

**Micro-mindfulness** (3 times a day):

1. **Morning**: Upon waking, lie in bed and notice 3 breaths.
2. **Noon**: Before eating lunch, pause for 10 seconds and feel the hunger.
3. **Night**: Before sleeping, review 3 moments from the day.

**Total time**: < 5 minutes.

**Effect**: Trains the ability to "open awareness" at any given moment.

---

### ¬ß1.5 Day 30: Assessment

**Compare**: Day 30 vs Day 1 journal ratings.

**Questions**:
- Any change in stress levels? (Expected: slight decrease)
- Quality of focus? (Expected: slight increase)
- Compassion? (Likely unchanged, this takes longer)

**If**:
- ‚úÖ **Improvement**: Continue, consider increasing to 15-20 minutes.
- ‚ùå **No Improvement**: 
  - Did you really do it every day?
  - How was the quality? (Too much distraction?)
  - Consider changing techniques or seeking guidance.

## ¬ß4 Integration into Life

### ¬ß4.1 Practice at Work

**Misconception**: "Practice" only happens on the cushion.

**Truth**: Work can be the **most powerful** training ground.

#### Mindful Work Techniques

**Technique 1: Task Boundary Awareness**

Every time you switch contexts (e.g., Email ‚Üí Meeting)‚Äî

1. **Pause** for 3 seconds.
2. **Notice** your current state (Tense? Anxious?).
3. **Breathe** once.
4. **Start** the new task.

**Effect**: Prevents "autopilot" mode.

---

**Technique 2: Difficult Conversations as Practice**

Next time you have a conflict/difficult conversation‚Äî

**Before**:
- Notice your defensiveness.
- Set an intention ("understand them" instead of "win").

**During**:
- Notice your emotions rising.
- Pause before responding (don't react).

**After**:
- Reflect: Where did I react? Where did I respond?

**SRT**: Conflict = High $\Psi_f$ = Golden opportunity for practice.

---

**Technique 3: Service as Practice**

Reframe your work as "service"‚Äî

- Not "I have to finish these tasks."
- But "I am helping X" (clients, colleagues, users).

**SRT**: 

$$\text{Service Framing} \implies d \uparrow$$

Even boring work, reframed as service ‚Üí Meaningfulness ‚Üë.

$$\text{Relationship} \implies \begin{cases}

#### Relationship Practice Techniques

**Technique 1: Mindful Listening**

Next time your partner/friend speaks‚Äî

**Do NOT**:
- Plan your response.
- Interrupt.
- "Fix" it.
- Give advice (unless asked).

**Do**:
- Be fully present.
- Notice your impulse to interrupt/fix.
- Just listen.

**Effect**: The other person feels truly heard‚Äîdeep connection.

---

**Technique 2: Triggers as Practice**

When your partner "triggers" you (anger, defensiveness)‚Äî

**Steps**:
1. **Notice** the trigger (physical tension).
2. **Pause** (don't react instantly).
3. **Breathe** (3 deep breaths).
4. **Inquire**: 
   - What old wound did this touch?
   - What was my $L_2$ expectation?
5. **Choose** your response (rather than reacting).

**SRT**: Trigger = Your unhealed $L_2$ is activated = Healing opportunity.

**Technique 3: Compassion Practice (For Partner)**

Every night, after your partner falls asleep‚Äî

1. Look at them.
2. Imagine their pain, fears, and wounds.
3. Silently say: "May you be happy, may you be safe, may you be free."
4. Feel compassion arise.

**Effect**: Maintains $d > 0$ even during difficult times.
