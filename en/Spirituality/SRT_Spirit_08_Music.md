**Common Misconception**: Music is the "art of hearing" (just as painting is the art of seeing).

**Deeper Truth**: Music is the **art of time**.

**Why?**

Painting and sculpture exist in **space**—you can:
- Stand far back
- Look closer
- View from different angles
- Dedicate any arbitrary amount of time to viewing it

Music exists in **time**—you must:
- Experience it according to its own temporal flow
- You cannot "skip" (without destroying the structure)
- You cannot "rewind" (time passes in real time)

**SRT**:

$$\text{Music} : T_{\text{selective}} = T_{\text{music}} \neq T_{\text{clock}}$$

### §4.2 Music Addiction

**Phenomenon**: "Music addiction"—
- Cannot stop listening to a specific song.
- Listening to music replaces other essential activities.
- Withdrawal symptoms?

**Mechanism**: Music → Dopamine → Reward circuit.

**But**: 
- Most music use is **adaptive** (enhances emotional regulation).
- In a minority of cases, it becomes **escapism** (e.g., PTSD patients using music to numb themselves).

**Diagnosis**: 
- Is function impaired? (Affecting work, relationships)
- Is it compulsive? (Inability to control)

**If yes** → Intervention may be needed (treating the underlying anxiety/depression).

---

### §4.3 "Earworms"

**Phenomenon**: A song gets stuck in the mind—looping endlessly.

**Scientific Name**: Involuntary Musical Imagery (INMI)

**Prevalence**: 90%+ of people experience it.

**Triggers**:
- Recently heard.
- Simple, repetitive melody.
- Emotional arousal.
- Occurs during boredom or distraction.

**Neurology**: Spontaneous activation of the auditory cortex (even without external music).

**Remedies**:
- Listen to the full song ("completion" effect).
- Chew gum (occupies the motor cortex tied to vocalization).
- Think of another piece of music.
- Accept it (resisting it often reinforces it).
