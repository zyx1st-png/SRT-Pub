## §1 The Precision Engineering of Buddhist Practice

### §1.1 Mindfulness Training of the Four Foundations of Mindfulness (Satipaṭṭhāna)

Buddhism's core cultivation technique is the "Four Foundations of Mindfulness"—continuous awareness of body, feelings, mind, and phenomena (Dhammas).

**Why is this so important?**

Because "Ignorance" (Avidya) is not "not knowing a certain fact," but **unawareness of the selection process itself**.

$$\text{Avidya} = \text{Unconscious Automation of } \hat{G}_\theta$$

We do not just "not know suffering", but we do not know **how suffering arises**—that is, we are unaware of the $L_0 \to L_1$ selection mechanism.

**Satipaṭṭhāna through the lens of SRT**:

#### 1. Mindfulness of the Body (Kāyānupassanā)

Observing the body—not "what the body is," but "how the body is experienced."

$$\text{Observation} : \text{Direct awareness of } \frac{\partial L_1^{\text{body}}}{\partial t}$$

**Practice**: Breath observation, body scanning, mindfulness in walking, standing, sitting, lying down.

**Goal**: Realizing that the "body" is not a fixed entity, but a flowing process.

#### 2. Mindfulness of Feelings (Vedanānupassanā)

Observing feelings—pain, pleasure, neither-pain-nor-pleasure.

$$\text{Observation} : \text{Real-time monitoring of } \frac{\partial \Psi_f}{\partial L_1}$$

**Key Insight**: Feelings are not "attributes of things," but the evaluative reactions of $\hat{G}_\theta$ to $L_1$.

**Practice**: Noting the arising, presence, and passing away of feelings.

#### 3. Mindfulness of the Mind (Cittānupassanā)

Observing mental states—greed, aversion, delusion, etc.

$$\text{Observation} : \text{Dynamic tracking of } \vec{v}_\theta(t)$$

**Practice**: Awareness of the flow of emotions, thoughts, and intentions.

**Breakthrough**: Realizing that the "mind" is not the master, but also an object being observed.

#### 4. Mindfulness of Dhammas/Mental Objects (Dhammānupassanā)

Observing principles/laws—Five Aggregates, Twelve Links of Dependent Origination, Four Noble Truths.

$$\text{Observation} : \text{The structural patterns of } \hat{G}[L_0 \to L_1]$$

**Practice**: Real-time observation of dependent origination occurring.

**Ultimate**: Direct intuition of "non-self"—there is no independent $\hat{G}_\theta$, only a process.

## §2 The Four School Debates in Vedanta

### §2.1 Monism vs. Dualism vs. Qualified Monism

#### (1) Non-Dualism (Advaita) — Shankara

$$\text{Atman} = \text{Brahman} \quad \text{(Absolute Identity)}$$

**Analogy**: Wave = Ocean

**Question**: Then why is there a "sense of separation"?
**Answer**: The veiling of Maya (Illusion).

**Answer**: Not ontological union, but relational union (like Christianity).

$$\text{Atman} \subset \text{Brahman} \quad \text{(Part-Whole)}$$

**Analogy**: Cell ⊂ Body

**SRT Reconciliation**: It depends on the level of observation—
