## §1 The Revolutionary Insights of Daoist Philosophy

### §1.1 The Metalanguage Trap at the Opening of the *Dao De Jing*

> "The Dao that can be told is not the eternal Dao; The name that can be named is not the eternal name."

This is not deliberate mystification, but a precise statement about the limits of language.

**Traditional Misreading**: "The Dao is too mysterious to be spoken of."

**Correct Understanding**: "Once you use language to speak of the 'Dao', you are no longer talking about the Dao itself, but about the concept of the Dao."

**Why?**

Because language ($L_2$) is the product of selection—

$$L_2 = \{\text{Concepts}\} = \hat{G}[\text{Past Selections}]$$

Whereas the Dao ($\hat{G}$) is the condition that makes selection possible. Using the product to define the producer inevitably leads to circularity.

**Analogy**:
- Trying to define the "act of mapping" using a "map"
- Trying to define "language itself" using a "dictionary"
- Trying to define the "compiler" using "program output"

**Modern Philosophy**: Wittgenstein's "language games", Heidegger's "ontological difference" (between Being and beings)—all deal with the same problem.

**Key**: Wu Wei is not "incompetence", but "effortlessness"—when you completely align with the natural flow, action becomes effortless.

## §3 Deep Analysis of Near-Death Experiences (NDE)

**Ancient**: NDEs seen as miracles or demonic possession

**Enlightenment**: Dismissed as superstition
**Early 20th Century**: Freudian psychoanalysis (projection of death anxiety)

**2000s**: Intervention of neuroscience (Blanke, Parnia)

### §3.3 The Neuroscience of Out-of-Body Experiences (OBE)

**Controversy**: Is OBE "the soul truly leaving" or a "brain-constructed hallucination"?

**Modern Evidence**:

#### 1. The Role of the Temporoparietal Junction (TPJ)

Blanke et al. (2002) found: electrical stimulation of the right TPJ can induce OBE.

**Mechanism**: The TPJ integrates somatic sensation and spatial information—when it dysfunctions, the brain may construct a "displaced self".

#### 2. Default Self-Location

$$\text{Self-Location} = \int (\text{Visual} + \text{Vestibular} + \text{Proprioceptive}) \, d\text{sensory}$$

When inputs conflict or are absent (as in near-death), the brain might "default" to a remembered bird's-eye perspective.

#### 3. Reproducible OBEs

- **VR Technology** (Ehrsson, 2007): Inducing OBE-like experiences via visual-tactile mismatch
- **Lucid Dreaming Techniques**: Training can actively trigger OBEs

**Conclusion**: The neural mechanisms of OBE are largely understood—there is no need to postulate a "soul."

**But**: This cannot explain rare cases of "accurate information acquisition" (e.g., Pam Reynolds).

### §3.4 Neural Explanation of the Tunnel and Light

**Classic Explanation**: Retinal hypoxia leads to the preservation of central vision while peripheral vision is lost → Tunnel effect.

**Problem**: Blind people also report "tunnel" experiences (Ring & Cooper, 1999).

**Better Model**: 
1. Cortical Disinhibition
2. Excitation-inhibition imbalance
3. "Noise" interpreted as "light"

**SRT**: "Light" may be the "raw presentation" of the $L_0$ information field in the absence of $L_2$ filtering.
