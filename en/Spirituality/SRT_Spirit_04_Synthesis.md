**The Theodicy Problem**: If God is omnibenevolent, omnipotent, and omniscient, why does evil exist?

$$\text{Evil} \neq \text{Force} \quad \text{but} \quad \text{Trapped Configuration}$$

## §5 The Paradoxes of God and Apophatic Theology

### §5.1 Why Must God Be Paradoxical?

**Logical Analysis**:

Suppose God can be completely described by $L_2$:

$$\exists P \in L_2 : P(\text{God}) = \text{Complete Description}$$

But $L_2$ is the product of $\hat{G}$:

$$L_2 = \hat{G}[\text{Past Operations}]$$

$$P(\text{God}) = \hat{G}[\text{Past}](\text{God})$$

This is a circular definition → It cannot be complete.

**Conclusion**: Any attempt to completely describe God will yield paradoxes or incompleteness.

### §5.2 SRT Interpretation of Classic Paradoxes

#### Paradox 1: Can God create a rock so heavy he cannot lift it?

**Traditional Dilemma**:
- If yes → there is something he cannot lift → not omnipotent.
- If no → there is something he cannot do → not omnipotent.

**SRT Solution**:

The question itself is an $L_2$-level self-referential paradox—

$$\text{"Unmovable by Omnipotent"} = \text{Logical Contradiction}$$

God's "omnipotence" does not include logical contradictions—this is not a limitation, but the definition of a "contradiction."

**Analogy**: "Creating a square circle" is not a limit on omnipotence; the definitions of "square" and "circle" make it impossible.

---

#### Paradox 2: If God knows the future, how can there be free will?

**Traditional Dilemma**:
- God knows my choices tomorrow.
- So my choices today are already "known."
- Therefore, they are not "free."

**SRT Solution**:

"Knowing" does not equal "determining"—

$$\text{Knowledge}(L_0) \neq \text{Causation}(L_1)$$

God "knows" all possible branches of $L_0$, but does not pre-select which one is actualized → $\hat{G}_\theta$ still has room for choice.

**Analogy**: A physicist "knows" all possible trajectories, but does not dictate which path a particle takes.
