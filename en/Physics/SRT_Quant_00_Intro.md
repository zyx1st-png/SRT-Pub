# Part B: Expanded Context (English)

## 1. Why a new interpretation layer

Quantum theory predicts accurately but interpretation remains fragmented around three core tensions:
- collapse criterion,
- nonlocality framing,
- quantum-classical boundary.

SRT reframes each in a single operator-ontology language.

## 2. Reinterpretation of classic experiments

### 2.1 Double slit
Superposition is uncollapsed latent structure; detection is selection anchoring.

### 2.2 Delayed choice
Choice at readout can be modeled as current anchoring constraining admissible reconstructed history paths.

### 2.3 Schrodinger cat
Macroscopic stabilization is fast convergence under strong `L_2` constraints.

### 2.4 Quantum eraser
Removing correlation records can reopen interference conditions by altering effective anchoring structure.

### 2.5 Bell/Aspect-type correlations
Correlations are expected from shared `L_0` structure rather than direct `L_1` transfer.

### 2.6 Zeno effect
Frequent selection/update can suppress state drift by repeated projection-like resets.
