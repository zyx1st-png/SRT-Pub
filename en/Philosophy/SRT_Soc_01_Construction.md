## §1. The Physics of the Objectification Paradox

### 1.1 The Core Dilemma of Berger and Luckmann

In *The Social Construction of Reality* (1966), Peter Berger and Thomas Luckmann articulated sociology's fundamental paradox:

> **Paradox**: Society is entirely created by humans (subjective), but why does it not only feel objective, but also recursively control humans (e.g., laws, morals, institutions)?

**Three Empirical Observations**:  
1. We "know" rules are man-made (e.g., driving on the right).
2. We "feel" rules are objective (violations trigger intense discomfort).
3. We are "forced" to obey rules (even if we privately disagree).

The two major camps of mainstream sociology fail to perfectly explain this:
- **Durkheimianism**: Treats society as a physical fact → ignores agency.
- **Weberianism**: Emphasizes subjective meaning → cannot explain coercive force.

---

### 1.2 The SRT Hysteresis Resolution

SRT introduces the **physics concept of the hysteresis loop** to explain objectification:

**The Three-Stage Process**:

$$\text{Stage 0 (Externalization)}: \hat{G}_1 \text{ chooses } \sigma_1 \in L_0 \quad \text{(Free selection)}$$
$$\text{Stage 1 (Habituation)}: \hat{G}_1 \text{ repeats } \sigma_1 \implies \Psi_f(\sigma_1) \downarrow \quad \text{(Energy well deepens)}$$
$$\text{Stage 2 (Objectification)}: \hat{G}_2 \text{ perceives } \sigma_1 \in L_2 \quad \text{(Newcomer sees "natural law")}$$

**Key Insight**: Objectivity = **Historical Sedimentation**.

The "hardness" of an institution is proportional to:

### 2.2 SRT Interpretation of Mortality Salience Experiments

**Result**: The MS group showed a significant shift toward conservatism:
- Support for harsh penalties (+23%)
- Xenophobia (+31%)
- Adherence to tradition (+18%)

**SRT Mechanistic Explanation**:

$$\text{MS} \uparrow \implies \Psi_f(L_0 \to L_1) \uparrow \implies \hat{G} \text{ contracts } \theta$$

When the threat of death appears (chaotic $L_0$ leakage), the operator instinctively:
1. **Narrows the selection scope** ($d \downarrow$) → Cares only for the self and in-group.
2. **Fortifies the $L_2$ shield** → Clings to "absolute truth" (religion, nationalism).
3. **Rejects heterogeneous $L_2$** → Attacks other cultures (because they expose the falsity of the shield).

This is not "irrationality," but the **thermodynamically optimal strategy**.

## §3. The Tyranny of Language and the Resurrection of Sapir-Whorf

> "The limits of my language mean the limits of my world." (Tractatus 5.6)

**SRT Precision**:

$$L_0^{accessible}(\theta_{language}) = \{\sigma : \exists \text{ word for } \sigma\}$$

$$L_0^{accessible} \subset L_0^{full}$$

**Corollary**: If there is no vocabulary in the language to describe a certain state, even though the state exists in $L_0$, the operator **is almost unable to stably select it**.

### 3.2 Color Experiment: The Cultural Difference of the Blue-Green Boundary

**Experiment** (Kay & Kempton, 1984):  
English has a "blue/green" boundary; Russian has a "голубой(light blue) / синий(dark blue)" boundary.

Subjects sorted color chips:
- English speakers hesitated +40% longer near the blue/green boundary.
- Russian speakers hesitated +35% longer near the light/dark blue boundary.

**SRT Explanation**:  
Language carves **potential barriers** into the $L_0$ color space:

$$\Psi_f(\text{cross boundary}) \propto - \log P(\text{word exists})$$

No word = high barrier = difficulty crossing.

### 3.3 The Extreme Case of Radical Sapir-Whorf

**The Pirahã Language** (Everett, 2005):  
- No number words (only "few" and "many")
- No color words

- **No abstract concept of time** (no "tomorrow", "last year")

**Neurological Testing**: Pirahã speakers could not complete a simple "save this for tomorrow" task—not out of unwillingness, but because they **could not conceptualize** it.

**SRT Conclusion**:  
Language not only "influences" thought; in extreme cases, it **constitutes the topological structure of thought**. For those without temporal vocabulary, their $L_0$ time dimension is **flattened**.
